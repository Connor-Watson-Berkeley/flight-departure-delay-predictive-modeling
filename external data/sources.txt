DATA SOURCES FOR EXTERNAL EVENT DATA
=====================================

GOVERNMENT SHUTDOWN DATA
------------------------
Source: Wikipedia - Government shutdowns in the United States
URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_shutdowns_in_the_United_States

Source: Congressional Research Service - Past Government Shutdowns: Key Resources
URL: https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R41759

Source: Al Jazeera - A history of US government shutdowns
URL: https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/10/1/a-history-of-us-government-shutdowns-every-closure-and-how-long-it-lasted

Key Findings:
- December 22, 2018 - January 25, 2019 shutdown (35 days)
  Impact: Partial shutdown affecting FAA operations and TSA, though both deemed essential services
- October 1, 2025 - November 12, 2025 shutdown (43 days)
  Impact: Longest government shutdown in US history; significant federal disruptions
  Source: https://usafacts.org/articles/government-shutdown-2025-what-to-know/

Note: Current analysis uses 2015-2021 flight data, so only 2018-2019 shutdown overlaps.
Filter by date when merging with flight dataset.

FAA OPERATIONAL EVENTS DATA
----------------------------
Source: FAA Data Portal
URL: https://www.faa.gov/data

Source: OPSNET (Operations Network)
URL: https://www.aspm.faa.gov/opsnet/sys/main.asp
Note: Contains air traffic operations and delay data from FY 1990 onwards

Source: NAS Status (National Airspace System)
URL: https://nasstatus.faa.gov/

Source: News reports on ATC staffing during 2018-2019 shutdown
Note: January 2019 saw increased sick-outs and staffing shortages at major facilities

Key Finding: No major FAA strikes during 2015-2021 timeframe
Note: The 1981 PATCO strike is the most significant ATC strike but falls outside our data range
